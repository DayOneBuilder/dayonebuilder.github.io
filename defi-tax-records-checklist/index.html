DeFi tax records checklist

The worst time to figure out what records you need for DeFi taxes is after you have already bridged across chains, farmed three protocols, claimed an airdrop, and forgotten where half the source data lives.

If you want tax prep to be annoying instead of catastrophic, start by collecting the records that stop you from rebuilding your year from memory.

The records most people need first

Wallet and account list

Every wallet, exchange account, and chain you touched. If one source is missing, the whole story gets weird fast.

Transaction exports

CSV exports, explorer links, or wallet history for swaps, transfers, staking, LP moves, claims, and disposals.

Protocol activity notes

Which protocol you used, what the action was, and why the movement happened. Raw transactions do not always explain intent.

Price and value context

Receipt date, token amount, and estimated value at the time for things like rewards, airdrops, or weird token receipts.

Events that usually cause cleanup pain

  • bridges that look like disposals if you do not track both sides
  • LP deposits and withdrawals without a clear before-and-after record
  • staking rewards that were received but never labeled
  • airdrop claims with no saved date or fair value note
  • wallet-to-wallet transfers that break cost basis if they are treated like sales

A practical way to keep this sane

You do not need to solve your entire tax treatment in one sitting. You do need one place to map sources, classify common DeFi events, and flag the gaps before they spread into every later step.

That is usually enough to stop the worst failure mode: opening ten tabs, exporting random CSV files, and still not knowing what is missing.

What to collect before you start any cleanup pass

  • a wallet and source inventory
  • a simple event classification sheet
  • a checklist for records you still need to pull
  • notes on uncertain transactions you need to review later
