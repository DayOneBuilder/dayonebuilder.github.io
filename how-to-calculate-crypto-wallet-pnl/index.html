How to calculate crypto wallet P&L

Wallet P&L gets messy fast because people mix up capital in, capital out, current holdings value, and cost basis, then try to force all of that into one number.

If you want a rough but useful wallet-level answer, split the job into a few parts instead of pretending one screenshot or dashboard number explains everything.

Start with the numbers that matter

Total deposits

How much capital you put into the wallet over time.

Total withdrawals

How much capital you already pulled out.

Current holdings value

What the wallet still holds right now at current prices.

Current holdings cost basis

What the assets still sitting in the wallet originally cost you.

One rough way to think about wallet P&L

  • Total P&L = withdrawals + current holdings value - total deposits
  • Net capital still in play = total deposits - withdrawals
  • Unrealized P&L = current holdings value - current holdings cost basis
  • Realized P&L = total P&L - unrealized P&L

That is not full tax accounting, but it is enough to stop guessing whether the wallet is actually up, flat, or bleeding.

Where people usually get this wrong

  • they treat wallet transfers like profit
  • they count deposits but forget withdrawals
  • they use current value without checking cost basis
  • they try to read realized and unrealized gains from one single portfolio number

What this helps you answer

Is this wallet actually profitable?

You can get a rough answer without rebuilding every trade from scratch first.

How much capital is still exposed?

That matters when you are deciding whether the current holdings are carrying too much of the result.

Is the gain already realized or mostly paper?

That distinction matters more than people admit.

Does the dashboard number even make sense?

A simple calculator is often enough to catch obvious nonsense.
