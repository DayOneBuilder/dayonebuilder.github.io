Do LP fees actually cover impermanent loss?

Sometimes yes. A lot of the time, no. The problem is that people compare a juicy fee APR to nothing in particular, then act surprised when the position still trails simple hold after the pair moves hard.

If you provide liquidity in a 50/50 pool, the real question is not "is the APR good?" It is "after the price move, did this LP beat just holding the assets?"

Why this trips people up

APR looks concrete

It feels like a clean number. That makes it easy to over-trust.

Price moves do the real damage

If one side runs or dumps hard enough, the LP math can overpower the fee story fast.

People compare to entry price, not hold

The honest baseline is usually simple hold, not your original deposit value.

Fees are often hand-waved

Expected fees, real fees, and incentives are not the same thing. That gap matters.

The simple check that matters

Before entering a volatile pair, or after sitting in one for a while, compare three numbers:

  • what simple hold would be worth now
  • what the LP position would be worth before fees
  • what the LP would be worth after the fees or incentives you actually expect

If the post-fee LP still trails hold by more than you are comfortable with, the APR was never the real answer.

When fees usually do cover the gap

  • the pair stayed in a relatively tight range
  • volume stayed high enough to generate real fees
  • you were not relying on fantasy yield or short-lived incentives
  • the time in the pool was long enough for those fees to stack up

When fees usually do not

  • one token moved hard and the other became the bag you did not want more of
  • APR looked high because incentives were temporary
  • volume dried up while price kept moving
  • you never checked the hold baseline and just trusted the dashboard headline

A quick example

Say you start with $10,000 in a 50/50 pool and one side doubles. Your LP probably did fine in absolute terms, but that is not the same as keeping up with hold. If the fee income does not close the gap, the LP still lost to the simpler choice.

That is the part people skip. They see green numbers and assume the trade was good. Sometimes it was only less bad than it looked.
